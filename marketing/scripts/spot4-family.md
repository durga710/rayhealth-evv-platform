# Spot 4 — Family ("Closer to the people you love.")

**Length:** 30s
**Word count target:** ~70
**Tone:** Tender, calm. Reassurance without melodrama. Avoid the "sick parent" cliché.
**Voice direction:** Warm female, 35-55, with gravity. Reads slow. Not a salesperson voice.
**Channels:** Connected TV (Roku/Hulu) for 55+ household targeting, hospital discharge-planner referral video, GP waiting-room loop.

---

## Voiceover

When mom comes home from the hospital, you want to know she's not alone. RayHealth quietly connects the family to the care. See when the caregiver arrives. Know the visit is complete. Read the notes from this morning. No phone tag. No "did anyone go today." Just steady, verifiable care — and a little peace of mind, too.

**Word count:** 64.

## Music direction

Solo piano, slow, no swell. Think a Hammock or Olafur Arnalds piece — not Yann Tiersen which is too "movie."
Artlist search: "gentle piano emotional restraint" BPM 60-75.

---

## Notes

CRITICAL — do not over-emote. The temptation in family-care advertising is to push the tears button. Resist. This audience (45-65, planning care for aging parents) is sophisticated and reads emotional manipulation immediately. The hook is the daughter's shoulders dropping at 21s — quiet relief, not happy-cry. Direct the talent accordingly.
