**NOTICE: This is a template. Have a licensed attorney review and finalize before use.**

This Business Associate Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of __________ ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Covered Entity:** ________________________________, a __________ [entity type] ("Agency")

**Business Associate:** RayHealth Technologies, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("RayHealth")

## 1. Background

Agency is a "covered entity" as defined under HIPAA. RayHealth provides electronic visit verification (EVV) software services to Agency that require RayHealth to create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information (PHI) on Agency's behalf. This Agreement sets out the terms under which RayHealth may use and disclose such PHI.

## 2. Definitions

Terms used but not defined here have the meanings given in 45 C.F.R. §§ 160.103, 164.103, 164.304, 164.402, and 164.501.

- **PHI**: Protected Health Information as defined at 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.
- **ePHI**: Electronic PHI.
- **HIPAA Rules**: The HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart E) and the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart C), as amended.
- **Services**: The EVV platform and related services described in the Master Service Agreement between the parties.

## 3. Permitted Uses and Disclosures by RayHealth

3.1 RayHealth may use or disclose PHI only as necessary to perform the Services, as required by law, or as otherwise permitted under this Agreement.

3.2 RayHealth may use PHI for the proper management and administration of its business or to carry out its legal responsibilities, provided such use does not violate the HIPAA Rules.

3.3 RayHealth may disclose PHI to its subcontractors that are themselves bound by written agreements that impose obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement.

3.4 RayHealth shall not use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate the HIPAA Rules if done by Agency.

3.5 RayHealth shall not sell PHI, use PHI for marketing purposes, or use PHI to conduct independent research without Agency's prior written authorization.

## 4. Obligations of RayHealth

4.1 **Safeguards.** RayHealth shall implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI as required by 45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart C.

4.2 **Subcontractors.** RayHealth shall enter into a written agreement with any subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on RayHealth's behalf, imposing the same restrictions, conditions, and requirements that apply to RayHealth under this Agreement.

4.3 **Breach Notification.** RayHealth shall notify Agency without unreasonable delay, and in no case later than 60 days after discovery, of any Breach of Unsecured PHI. Notification shall include: (a) identities of affected individuals; (b) a description of the PHI involved; (c) the date of breach and discovery; (d) steps RayHealth is taking to investigate, mitigate, and prevent future occurrences.

4.4 **Security Incidents.** RayHealth shall report to Agency any Security Incident of which it becomes aware. Reports of incidents that do not rise to a Breach may be made on a quarterly basis.

4.5 **Individual Rights.** To the extent PHI is maintained in a Designated Record Set, RayHealth shall, upon Agency's written request: (a) provide access to PHI; (b) amend PHI; (c) account for disclosures. RayHealth shall respond within 15 business days.

4.6 **Minimum Necessary.** RayHealth shall make reasonable efforts to use, disclose, or request only the minimum PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

4.7 **HHS Audits.** RayHealth shall make its internal practices, books, and records available to the Secretary of HHS for purposes of determining HIPAA compliance.

## 5. Obligations of Agency

5.1 Agency shall notify RayHealth of any limitation in its Notice of Privacy Practices that may affect RayHealth's use or disclosure of PHI.

5.2 Agency shall notify RayHealth of any changes in, or revocation of, permission by individuals to use or disclose PHI.

5.3 Agency shall not request that RayHealth use or disclose PHI in a manner that would not be permissible under the HIPAA Rules if done by Agency.

## 6. Term and Termination

6.1 **Term.** This Agreement is effective on the Effective Date and remains in force until terminated.

6.2 **Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate upon written notice if the other materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 30 days of written notice.

6.3 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, RayHealth shall return or destroy all PHI in its possession if feasible. Where return or destruction is infeasible, RayHealth shall extend the protections of this Agreement to any retained PHI and limit further use to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible.

## 7. Miscellaneous

7.1 **Amendment.** The parties shall amend this Agreement as necessary to maintain compliance with HIPAA and other applicable law.

7.2 **Interpretation.** This Agreement shall be interpreted as broadly as necessary to comply with HIPAA. In any conflict between this Agreement and the Master Service Agreement, this Agreement controls with respect to PHI.

7.3 **No Third-Party Beneficiaries.** This Agreement confers no rights on any third party.

7.4 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware.

7.5 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Master Service Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement of the parties with respect to PHI.
